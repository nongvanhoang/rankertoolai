7 Best Grammarly Alternatives in 2026 (Tested & Ranked)

Updated June 30, 2026 · 14 min read · Tested by RankerToolAI team

Why People Switch from Grammarly

  • Price vs features: Grammarly Premium at $12/mo is affordable, but QuillBot and ProWritingAid offer comparable or better features — particularly for paraphrasing and long-form editing — at lower prices
  • Paraphrasing capability: Grammarly's rewriting suggestions are minimal compared to QuillBot's dedicated paraphrasing modes and tone options
  • Fiction and creative writing: Grammarly applies business-writing standards (active voice, concision) that can conflict with intentional stylistic choices in fiction and creative work
  • Citation assistance: academic writers need plagiarism checking and citation formatting that Grammarly's standard plan doesn't fully support
Before switching: Grammarly's free plan covers basic grammar and spelling in 500+ apps — most users never need the Premium tier. Before switching, confirm you've actually tried the features that Premium adds: clarity suggestions, tone detection, and goal-setting. If you only need grammar checking, the free plan may already meet your needs. Try Grammarly →

QuillBot — Best for AI Paraphrasing and Rewriting

★★★★☆
8.2/10 Top Pick

QuillBot is the most capable Grammarly alternative for writers who need to rephrase, restructure, and rewrite existing text. Its 7 paraphrase modes — Standard, Fluency, Formal, Simple, Creative, Shorten, and Expand — provide control over output style that Grammarly's one-size-fits-all suggestions can't match. The Fluency mode eliminates grammatical errors while preserving your voice; Formal mode adds academic or professional register; Shorten condenses wordy passages without losing meaning.

QuillBot's free plan is significantly more generous than Grammarly's free tier: 125 words per paraphrase, the summarizer tool, grammar checker, and the Co-Writer (AI writing assistant) are all available without payment. The Premium plan at $9.95/mo ($3.33/mo on annual billing) unlocks unlimited paraphrase length, all 7 modes, a plagiarism checker, and a citation generator — comprehensive tools for academic writers. QuillBot Premium at $9.95/mo is cheaper than Grammarly Premium at $12/mo and provides superior paraphrasing capability. The grammar checking accuracy is comparable to Grammarly, though Grammarly's browser extension coverage across 500+ apps is broader than QuillBot's.

✅ Pros vs Grammarly

  • 7 paraphrase modes for controlled rewriting — no equivalent in Grammarly
  • Free plan more generous than Grammarly free tier
  • Summarizer tool condenses long documents automatically
  • Plagiarism checker and citation generator on Premium
  • $9.95/mo Premium vs Grammarly's $12/mo — $25/year cheaper

❌ Cons vs Grammarly

  • Browser extension coverage narrower than Grammarly's 500+ apps
  • Grammar checking accuracy comparable but not better than Grammarly
  • No tone detection or goal-setting features like Grammarly Premium
Starting Price: Free / $9.95/mo Try QuillBot →

ProWritingAid — Best for Authors and Long-Form Writers

★★★★☆
8.0/10

ProWritingAid is purpose-built for authors and long-form writers who need more than grammar checking. Its 25+ writing reports analyze pacing, sentence length variation, dialogue tag frequency, sticky phrases, over-used words, and readability — feedback that Grammarly's business-writing model doesn't provide. For novelists, memoirists, and long-form non-fiction writers, ProWritingAid's genre-aware analysis addresses the specific challenges of book-length manuscripts.

ProWritingAid integrates with Word, Google Docs, Scrivener, Final Draft, and Open Office — covering the full range of tools that serious writers use. Its Manuscript Review feature runs a comprehensive analysis of your entire book-length document in one pass, identifying patterns across chapters that sentence-by-sentence tools miss. The free plan allows 500 words per analysis. Premium at $10/mo (or $79/year lifetime option) unlocks unlimited document length and all 25+ reports. For professional writers who find Grammarly's suggestions too generic or business-oriented, ProWritingAid's depth of craft-level feedback is the superior alternative.

✅ Pros vs Grammarly

  • 25+ writing reports including pacing, dialogue, and readability analysis
  • Manuscript-level analysis identifies patterns across entire books
  • Integrates with Scrivener and Final Draft — tools serious authors use
  • Genre-aware feedback for fiction vs non-fiction styles
  • Lifetime plan option at $399 — one-time purchase

❌ Cons vs Grammarly

  • Less instant feedback than Grammarly's inline suggestions
  • Browser extension narrower than Grammarly's 500+ app coverage
  • Interface has a steeper learning curve for new users
Starting Price: Free / $10/mo Try ProWritingAid →

Hemingway Editor — Best for Clarity and Plain Language Writing

★★★★☆
7.8/10

Hemingway Editor takes a radically simpler approach than Grammarly: it color-codes your text to highlight complex sentences (purple), very complex sentences (red), passive voice (green), adverbs (blue), and weak phrases (yellow). The goal is ruthless clarity — shorter sentences, active voice, concrete language. For writers of blog posts, marketing copy, and non-fiction where readability is paramount, Hemingway's visual feedback is faster and more actionable than Grammarly's grammar-first approach.

The browser version of Hemingway Editor is completely free — paste text and get instant readability analysis at no cost. The desktop app at $19.99 is a one-time purchase with no subscription, adding distraction-free writing mode and direct publishing to Medium and WordPress. For users paying $12/mo for Grammarly Premium primarily to improve clarity and readability, Hemingway delivers superior clarity feedback at a one-time $19.99 cost — $124/year cheaper than Grammarly Premium annually. The tradeoff is that Hemingway doesn't catch grammar errors, spelling mistakes, or punctuation issues.

✅ Pros vs Grammarly

  • Free browser version — no account or payment required
  • One-time $19.99 desktop purchase — no subscription
  • Color-coded clarity feedback faster than Grammarly for readability editing
  • Readability grade level scoring for target audience calibration
  • Direct publishing to Medium and WordPress (desktop)

❌ Cons vs Grammarly

  • Does not check grammar, spelling, or punctuation — must be paired with another tool
  • No browser extension for inline writing in Gmail or Google Docs
  • No AI generation, paraphrasing, or rewriting capabilities
Starting Price: Free (browser) / $19.99 (desktop, one-time) Try Hemingway Editor →

★★★★☆
8.9/10

Writesonic shifts the approach from editing existing text to generating new content with quality built in from the start. Where Grammarly corrects what you write after the fact, Writesonic's Article Writer 6.0 generates SEO-optimized content structured around your keyword before editing is needed. For content marketers and bloggers who spend significant time editing AI-generated or rough-draft content, Writesonic reduces the volume of text that needs Grammarly-style correction.

Writesonic's Chatsonic AI assistant handles research, outline generation, and content refinement in a ChatGPT-like interface. The 100+ content templates cover blog posts, social media, ad copy, email sequences, and product descriptions. At $16/mo for the Small Team plan, Writesonic is more expensive than Grammarly Premium but replaces both writing and editing tools for content creators. For users who need both AI generation and grammar checking, using Writesonic for drafting and Grammarly Free for inline checking may be more cost-effective than paying for both Premium plans.

✅ Pros vs Grammarly

  • Article Writer 6.0 generates full SEO articles — reduces editing work
  • 100+ templates for marketing, social, email, and blog content
  • Chatsonic AI research assistant included
  • Real-time Google integration for factually accurate content
  • Strong for content marketing teams that produce high volume

❌ Cons vs Grammarly

  • $16/mo vs Grammarly's $12/mo — more expensive as a Grammarly replacement
  • AI-generated content still requires editing — doesn't eliminate the need for a grammar checker
  • Not suitable for editing existing human-written content
Starting Price: from $16/mo Try Writesonic →

8.4/10

Anyword addresses a use case Grammarly entirely ignores: predicting whether your marketing copy will actually convert. For marketers who use Grammarly to polish ad copy, email subject lines, and landing page headlines, Anyword provides a more specialized tool that adds performance prediction on top of quality checking.

Anyword's copy generation covers Facebook Ads, Google Ads, email subject lines, landing page headlines, and product descriptions — similar categories to where Grammarly users apply writing assistance. The performance scoring integrates with your ad account historical data to personalize predictions to your specific audience. At $39/mo, Anyword is more expensive than Grammarly Premium, but for paid media teams where a 1–2% conversion rate improvement on ad spend exceeds the tool cost, it provides ROI that pure grammar checking cannot. Anyword generates and scores; Grammarly corrects and polishes — they solve different problems for the same writer.

✅ Pros vs Grammarly

  • More valuable than grammar checking for paid media teams

❌ Cons vs Grammarly

Its free plan provides 10,000 characters per month across 40+ use cases — enough for regular social media posts, email drafts, and short-form content. The combination of Rytr free (generation) + Grammarly free (checking) covers both needs at zero cost.

Our Verdict: Best Grammarly Alternative

QuillBot is the best Grammarly alternative for most users: its AI paraphrasing and summarization capabilities significantly exceed Grammarly's rewriting suggestions, and its grammar checker is comparable in accuracy. Frequently Asked Questions

Is QuillBot better than Grammarly?

QuillBot is better specifically for paraphrasing and rewriting — its 7 paraphrase modes (Standard, Fluency, Formal, Creative, Shorten, Expand, Academic) provide granular control that Grammarly's suggestions don't match. Grammarly is better for inline grammar checking across all your apps via browser extension, and for professional tone consistency. Most serious writers use both for different tasks.

What is the best free Grammarly alternative?

QuillBot's free plan is the most capable free writing assistant: 125 words per paraphrase, summarizer, grammar checker, and a co-writer tool with no credit card required. Hemingway Editor is free in the browser version. LanguageTool offers a free plan with basic grammar checking in multiple languages.

Is ProWritingAid better than Grammarly for authors?

Yes, for long-form creative and non-fiction writing. ProWritingAid's 25+ writing reports include pacing analysis, dialogue tag overuse, sticky sentence detection, and readability across different genre expectations — feedback that Grammarly's business-writing-tuned model doesn't provide. Grammarly wins on speed and app coverage; ProWritingAid wins on depth of analysis for serious writers.

Does Grammarly work with Microsoft Word?

Yes. Grammarly has a Microsoft Word add-in (Windows and Mac) and a browser extension that works in Google Docs. QuillBot also has a Google Docs add-on and browser extension. ProWritingAid integrates with Word, Google Docs, Scrivener, and Final Draft.
