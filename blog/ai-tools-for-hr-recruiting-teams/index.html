A Realistic Hiring Workflow

Open a role: draft the job description in ChatGPT (10 min), check the salary band in Perplexity (5 min), and set up Zapier to auto-notify the hiring manager on new applicants. During interviews, run Otter.ai for notes. After each round, log candidate impressions in Notion so nothing depends on memory by the final round. Before sending an offer or rejection, run it through Grammarly's tone check — a rushed, cold-sounding rejection is one of the most common candidate-experience complaints and is easy to avoid.

A Note on AI and Hiring Decisions

These tools speed up drafting, organizing, and communication — they should not make or heavily weight actual hiring decisions. Many jurisdictions have specific regulations around AI use in hiring (automated resume screening and scoring in particular); check local requirements before using AI to filter or rank candidates, not just to draft communication.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can AI write a full job description on its own?

It gives a strong, well-structured first draft from a rough list of requirements, but a human should still review it for accuracy, inclusive language, and role-specific nuance before posting.

Is it legal to use AI to screen resumes?

Regulations vary by location — some jurisdictions require disclosure or audits for automated hiring tools. The tools in this guide are framed around drafting and organization, not automated candidate scoring; check local requirements before using any AI tool to rank or filter candidates.

What's the best free AI tool for small HR teams?

ChatGPT and Notion AI both have usable free tiers that cover job description drafting and basic candidate organization without spending anything. Otter.ai's free plan (600 minutes/month) covers most small teams' interview volume too.
