Surfer's live NLP Content Editor scoring was more precise and more consistently tied to ranking outcomes. Frase's automated brief generation was faster and cheaper for the research stage. Surfer SEO and Frase get lumped together as "content optimization tools," but they solve different parts of the workflow. Surfer's strength is live, in-editor scoring while writing. Frase's strength is automating the research and brief stage before writing starts. The real question isn't which is "better" in the abstract — it's which stage of your workflow actually causes the most friction today.

We took the same set of target keywords and ran the full workflow through both tools: building a content brief in Frase, then writing and optimizing the same article inside Surfer's Content Editor, tracking time spent and how directly each tool's guidance connected to actual ranking improvement.

Scored on:

  • Brief/research speed — time from keyword to usable outline.
  • Scoring precision — how actionable and specific the optimization guidance was.
  • Ranking correlation — whether following the guidance tracked with real ranking movement.

Where Frase Won

Brief generation speed. Frase's research automation is genuinely the best in class for speed — pulling competitor structure, PAA questions, and topic gaps into a usable brief in under two minutes, well ahead of doing the same research manually or trying to replicate it in Surfer.

Price. At $45/month versus Surfer's $89/month, Frase is roughly half the cost for teams that mainly need research and outline automation rather than deep live-editor scoring.

Combined true cost is close. Once you add Frase's $35/month AI writing add-on to get closer to full-draft generation, the total ($80/month) lands nearly at Surfer's price — at that point, Surfer's scoring precision is the stronger buy for the same money.

The real dividing line: If ranking content is your primary job, Surfer's scoring precision is worth the premium. If you mainly need fast research and outlines on a budget, Frase alone at $45/month covers that without the AI writing add-on.

When to Use Surfer SEO vs Frase

Verdict: Different Stages of the Same Workflow

Surfer SEO wins for teams whose core job is ranking content — the live scoring precision and stronger ranking correlation justify the higher price when optimization accuracy directly affects revenue.

Frase wins for budget-conscious teams and solo operators who mainly need fast, automated research and briefs — it's the better value at $45/month if deep live-editor scoring isn't the bottleneck in your workflow.

The honest recommendation: if budget allows, use Frase to build the brief and Surfer to optimize the final draft — the two-tool combination costs more but covers both ends of the workflow better than either alone. If you have to choose one, pick based on which stage — research or optimization — actually slows your team down today.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Surfer SEO better than Frase?
For content optimization and ranking, yes — Surfer SEO's live NLP scoring is more precise and more consistently tied to ranking improvements. For brief generation and research automation, Frase is faster and cheaper.
Can you use Surfer SEO and Frase together?
Yes. Some teams use Frase to generate research-backed briefs quickly, then use Surfer SEO's Content Editor to optimize the finished draft. This works well for teams that separate the briefing and optimization stages.
Which is cheaper, Surfer SEO or Frase?
Frase starts at $45/month versus Surfer SEO's $89/month — roughly half the price. Surfer's Content Editor scoring is more advanced, so the price gap reflects a real capability difference, not just positioning.
Does Frase have live content scoring like Surfer?
Frase has a Topic Score based on competitor topic coverage, similar in concept to Surfer's Content Score. In our testing, Surfer's NLP term-frequency scoring was more precise and more directly actionable for improving rankings.
