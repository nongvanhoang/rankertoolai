The Real Test: Every App, Every Day, for 30 Days

Wispr Flow's core promise is that it works everywhere — not a dedicated dictation app, but a system-wide layer over any text field. I tested this directly across email, Slack, Notion, and a code editor.

Method: For 30 days, I dictated instead of typed for every message over roughly two sentences — emails, Slack replies, Notion notes, and code comments — on both Mac and Windows.

Result: Dictation consistently ran at roughly 4x my typing speed, and AI Auto Edits caught the vast majority of filler words ("um," "like," false starts) without any manual cleanup. The biggest surprise was Command Mode — being able to say "delete that last sentence" or "make that bold" without touching the keyboard turned out to matter more day-to-day than raw dictation speed.

Roughly 4x typing speed held up consistently across apps, and AI Auto Edits removed the need for manual filler-word cleanup on almost every dictation. This is the tool's biggest strength.
