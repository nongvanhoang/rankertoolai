1. Rank Tracking Accuracy: Excellent ✅

This is SE Ranking's strongest suit, and it's not even close. I tracked 847 keywords across my five client projects, cross-referencing SE Ranking's reported positions against Google Search Console data every single week.

The result: 94% accuracy — SE Ranking matched GSC within 1-2 positions on 94% of tracked keywords. The 6% gap? Mostly volatile SERPs with rich snippets, local packs, or featured snippet fluctuations that make "position" ambiguous regardless of the tool you're using.

For comparison, this is roughly on par with what I was seeing from Semrush Pro. SE Ranking is not worse here — it's essentially equivalent.

A few things I appreciated specifically about SE Ranking's rank tracker:

  • Separate mobile/desktop tracking — critical as mobile rankings diverge more
  • Local rank tracking — down to city and zip code, useful for local SEO clients
  • Daily updates on paid plans — not every 3-7 days like some cheaper tools
  • Competitor rank tracking — track up to 5 competitors alongside your own rankings
  • Historical data — position history goes back to when you started tracking

The rank tracker UI is clean and the data exports to CSV without any friction. White-label reports are included, which matters for agencies sending clients weekly rank reports.

2. Keyword Research: Good Enough for Most Use Cases ✅

The honest take: SE Ranking's keyword database is smaller than Semrush or Ahrefs. They claim 5 billion+ keywords. Semrush claims 25 billion+. That gap is real — you'll occasionally search a long-tail term and get thin or no data.

But here's what I actually found in practice: for the keywords that matter to my clients, SE Ranking had solid data ~90% of the time. The gaps appeared almost exclusively on obscure long-tail terms with very low search volume — the kind where the data would be noisy and unreliable on any platform anyway.

What SE Ranking's keyword research does well:

  • Search volume, CPC, and competition scores for all major markets
  • Keyword difficulty scores that are actually calibrated well for ranking feasibility
  • Keyword grouping tool — clusters keywords by semantic similarity automatically
  • SERP analysis showing which pages rank and their estimated traffic
  • Related keywords and "People also ask" integration

The Keyword Grouper is actually a feature I didn't have in Semrush at this price point. It saved me probably 3-4 hours on my last content audit.

✓ Bottom line on keyword research: If you're a freelancer or small agency doing standard keyword research, SE Ranking covers 90% of what you need. If you need ultra-deep keyword data for enterprise-level campaigns, Semrush still has an edge. For most people, the 10% gap doesn't justify paying $74/month more.

3. Site Audit: Genuinely Thorough ✅

SE Ranking's site audit tool runs 130+ technical checks. I ran it on a mid-size e-commerce site (about 2,400 indexed pages) and it found 47 issues — including 9 that were legitimately critical and had been missed on previous audits with other tools.

The issues it caught that I found most valuable:

  • 3 pages with duplicate H1 tags (confirmed via manual check)
  • 11 product pages with thin content under 200 words
  • Core Web Vitals failures on 6 category pages (LCP over 4s)
  • Missing canonical tags on paginated URLs
  • 4 redirect chains (3+ hops) slowing crawl budget

The audit report categorizes issues by severity — critical, warnings, and notices — and gives actionable explanations for each one. Non-technical clients can actually read these reports and understand what needs to be fixed.

One standout: the on-page SEO checker lets you run per-page analysis against a target keyword, comparing your page to the top 10 ranking pages and giving specific optimization recommendations. It's similar to Semrush's On-Page SEO Checker and works well.

4. Backlink Analysis: The Weakest Area ⚠️

I want to be straight with you here because this is the biggest gap: SE Ranking's backlink database is noticeably smaller than Ahrefs or Semrush. On three of my client sites, SE Ranking was showing 30-45% fewer backlinks than Ahrefs reported. The links it was missing were primarily links from smaller, newer domains.

What this means in practice:

  • For basic backlink health checks, SE Ranking is fine — it catches the important links
  • For link prospecting and finding competitor link opportunities, it misses coverage
  • For disavow work where you need complete coverage, I'd still cross-reference Ahrefs
  • For tracking your own high-authority backlinks, it works well

If backlink analysis is a primary part of your service offering — client link building, link audits, outreach campaigns — SE Ranking's database is a real limitation. This is the one area where the price difference might not fully close the gap.

For everyone else doing rank tracking, keyword research, and technical audits? The backlink gap is an acceptable trade-off for $74/month in savings.

⚠️ Honest warning: If your primary SEO work involves link building campaigns, competitor link analysis, or detailed backlink audits, SE Ranking's smaller index will frustrate you. In that specific case, Ahrefs ($99/mo) remains the better tool despite the higher price.
