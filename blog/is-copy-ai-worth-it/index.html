The Real Test: Running Only the Free Plan for a Month

Copy.ai's headline feature isn't a specific writing capability — it's that the free plan never expires. That's a bigger claim than most "free trials," so it deserved a real test: could a small business actually run its marketing copy on the free plan alone for a full month, not just a demo session?

Method: Used only the free plan for a month, producing ad copy, email subject lines, product descriptions, and social captions for a small business, tracking where the 1-user, no-Workflows limits actually became a problem versus where they didn't matter.

Result: For short-form marketing copy — ads, emails, captions, product blurbs — the free plan's 90+ templates covered nearly every format needed with no word-count ceiling to worry about. The single-user limit only became a real constraint when a second person needed to collaborate on the same account; the missing Workflows automation only mattered once we wanted to chain steps (e.g., research → draft → repurpose) rather than run one-off prompts.

Nothing in a month of real short-form copy work required upgrading. The trigger for Starter was collaboration and automation, not running out of anything on the free plan itself.

FAQs

Is Copy.ai actually free forever?

Yes. Unlike most AI writing tools, Copy.ai's free plan has no trial expiration — it includes unlimited word generation for 1 user, permanently, with no credit card required.

What's the catch with the Copy.ai free plan?

The main limits are 1 user seat and no access to Workflows automation (multi-step content pipelines), which require the Starter plan at $36/month ($29/month billed annually).

Is Copy.ai good enough to skip paying entirely?

For solo creators and small businesses writing ads, emails, and product copy, yes — the free plan alone can cover most needs indefinitely. Teams needing collaboration or automation will want Starter.

How does Copy.ai compare to Jasper and Writesonic?

Copy.ai's free plan never expires, unlike Jasper's 7-day trial or Writesonic's capped 10,000 words. For long-form, brand-consistent content at scale, Jasper's paid plans are stronger — but Copy.ai wins on cost for short-form marketing copy.
