What AirOps Actually Does

AirOps is a no-code AI content operations platform that chains steps — keyword research, competitor scraping, AI drafting, internal linking, meta tag generation — into automated, repeatable workflows, plus AI search visibility tracking across ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews.

What AirOps Does Best

Coordinating multi-step content processes at scale

Where a single AI writing tool handles one article at a time, AirOps' workflows handle research through publishing across dozens or hundreds of URLs — the right tool specifically when coordination, not drafting, is the bottleneck.

Getting Started: Account Setup

Go to airops.com and sign up for the free Insights plan — 1,000 tasks/month, 1 user, basic templates, 1 Brand Kit, 5 Knowledge Base sources, and access to 30+ AI models. A 14-day trial of full Scale/Pro features is also available with no upfront payment.

The No-Code Workflow Builder

How to Build Your First Workflow

  1. Click New Workflow and start with the drag-and-drop canvas
  2. Add a data source step (a keyword list, a URL list, or a connected data provider)
  3. Chain processing steps — competitor scraping, AI drafting, internal linking, meta tag generation — in the order your content process actually follows
  4. Add a publishing step connected to your CMS if you want the workflow to publish automatically, or leave it as draft-only for manual review
  5. Test on a small batch (2-3 items) before running the full workflow at scale
Pro Tip

Budget real setup time for your first workflow, not your first hour

The steep learning curve is a consistently repeated criticism, but it's front-loaded — once a workflow is built and tested, running it again on new data is fast. Don't judge AirOps' value from the first build session alone.

Grids: Spreadsheet-Style Content Ops

Grids runs your workflows directly from a spreadsheet-style content calendar, useful for teams more comfortable planning in rows and columns than a visual workflow canvas.

How to Use Grids

  1. Open Grids and set up columns matching your content plan (URL, target keyword, status, assigned workflow)
  2. Trigger a workflow run directly from a row
  3. Track status across your entire content backlog from one view rather than switching between individual workflow runs

AI Search Visibility Tracking

How to Track AI Search Presence

  1. Go to AI Search Visibility and connect your domain
  2. AirOps monitors your presence across ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews for tracked queries
  3. Review which queries surface your content in AI-generated answers versus which don't, informing what to prioritize in future content workflows

This is a genuinely different signal from traditional rank tracking — as AI-generated answers increasingly satisfy searches without a click-through, visibility inside those answers matters even when your traditional ranking looks fine.

Integrations and Model Access

AirOps connects to 30+ AI models and 10+ data providers, plus 10+ CMS integrations for direct publishing.

  1. Go to Integrations and connect your CMS, data providers, and preferred AI models
  2. Select which model powers each workflow step — different steps may benefit from different models depending on the task

Full Workflow: Content Refresh at Scale

Here's a workflow for refreshing a large backlog of outdated content:

Step 1

Load your URL backlog into Grids

Import the list of pages needing a refresh as rows in your content calendar.

Step 2

Build (or reuse) a refresh workflow: competitor scrape, gap analysis, draft update, internal link check

Test on a handful of pages before running the full backlog.

Step 3

Run the workflow across the full backlog from Grids

Track progress and status across every URL from one view.

Step 4 — ongoing

Cross-check refreshed content against AI Search Visibility data

Confirm whether the refresh actually improved presence in AI-generated answers, not just traditional rankings.

Common Beginner Mistakes

  • Expecting a quick setup like a single-purpose writing tool. AirOps requires real upfront investment — budget accordingly rather than judging it after a rushed first attempt.
  • Running a new workflow on the full backlog before testing on a small sample. Validate on 2-3 items first to catch structural issues before they compound across hundreds of URLs.
  • Ignoring AI Search Visibility tracking. Traditional rank tracking alone misses a growing share of searches now satisfied by AI-generated answers.
