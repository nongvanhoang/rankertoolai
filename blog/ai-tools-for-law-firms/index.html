Where AI Actually Helps a Small Firm

The clearest wins are administrative: faster first drafts of routine correspondence, searchable transcripts instead of re-listening to recordings, and less manual data entry between intake and case management. Solo practitioners and small firms without dedicated support staff see the largest relative time savings, since these tools substitute for tasks a larger firm would delegate to paralegals or assistants.

A Note on AI and Legal Advice

Every tool in this guide speeds up drafting, research, and organization — none of them should be treated as a source of legal advice or a substitute for attorney judgment. AI-generated case citations have been documented to occasionally be fabricated ("hallucinated") in real filings, which is why the research tools here are framed as a starting point requiring verification in a proper legal database, not a final citation source. Always have a licensed attorney review AI-assisted drafts before they're used in client work or filed with a court.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can AI replace legal research?

No — general-purpose AI tools like Perplexity are useful as a starting point for concepts and general questions, but they are not a substitute for a proper legal research platform, and AI-generated case citations have been documented as occasionally fabricated. Always verify citations independently before filing anything.

Is it safe to use AI to draft client documents?

AI can produce a strong first draft, but every document needs a licensed attorney's review before it's sent to a client or filed. Treat AI output the same way you'd treat a draft from a junior associate — a starting point, not a final product.

What's the best AI tool for a solo practitioner?

Claude for drafting and Otter.ai for meeting/deposition transcription give the largest relative time savings for solo practitioners who don't have paralegal support, both with usable free tiers to start.
