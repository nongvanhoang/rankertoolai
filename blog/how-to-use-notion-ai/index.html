How to Use Notion AI in 2026: Step-by-Step Beginner Guide

Most Notion AI users only ever touch the "write with AI" button. The genuinely useful feature — workspace Q&A — is buried a click deeper. Here's how to use everything, including the parts most people miss.

What Notion AI Actually Does

Notion AI is a $10/month add-on to any Notion plan, built on a mix of Claude and GPT-4o under the hood. Its core advantage over a standalone chatbot is context — it has direct access to your existing pages, databases, and notes, so it can draft, summarize, and answer questions with awareness of content you've already written, without you pasting anything in manually.

What Notion AI Does Best

Answering questions across your entire workspace

The AI Q&A feature can search and answer questions across every page in your workspace using vector search — genuinely useful for teams with large wikis, SOPs, or knowledge bases where finding the right page manually takes real time.

Getting Started: Enabling AI

Notion AI isn't a separate product — it's an add-on activated from inside any existing Notion workspace. If you don't already have a Notion account, start on the free plan first, since AI needs an actual workspace with content in it to be useful.

  1. Open any page in your workspace
  2. Type space (the spacebar on an empty line) or highlight existing text to bring up the AI menu
  3. If prompted, activate the AI add-on for your workspace ($10/month per member)

Once activated, AI is available from any page — there's no separate app or dashboard to learn.

The AI Writer

How to Draft With AI

  1. On an empty line, press space to open the AI menu
  2. Choose an action: Write anything, Draft a blog post, Brainstorm ideas, and similar presets, or type a custom instruction
  3. Review the generated text — it appears as a draft block you can accept, edit, or discard
  4. Highlight any existing text and use Improve writing, Fix spelling & grammar, or Make shorter/longer for quick edits

Because the AI Writer runs inside your actual page, it naturally picks up on surrounding context — headings, existing paragraphs, linked databases — without you needing to explain what the document is about.

Autofill: AI Inside Databases

Autofill applies AI generation to database properties — useful for bulk-processing rows of a table rather than writing one page at a time.

How to Set Up Autofill

  1. Open a database and add a new property, setting its type to AI Autofill
  2. Choose an autofill action — summarize the page content, translate, extract key points, or a custom prompt
  3. Run it on a single row to test the output, then apply to the full database

A common use: a database of meeting notes where an Autofill column automatically generates a one-line summary for every entry, so you can scan the whole database without opening each page.

AI Q&A: The Hidden Gem

This is the feature most Notion AI subscribers never find. Instead of asking AI to write something new, Q&A searches your existing workspace and answers questions using content you've already written.

How to Use AI Q&A

  1. Click the Search icon (or press Ctrl/Cmd+K)
  2. Switch to the Ask AI tab
  3. Type a natural-language question about your workspace — "What did we decide about the Q3 budget?" or "Summarize last week's engineering standup notes"
  4. Notion AI searches relevant pages and returns an answer with links back to the source pages

For teams with large, sprawling workspaces, this replaces a manual search-and-skim process that otherwise costs real time whenever someone needs to find a decision or detail buried in old notes.

Full Workflow: Meeting Notes to Action Items

A workflow that gets real value from Notion AI in a single meeting cycle:

Step 1 — during the meeting

Take rough notes as normal

Don't worry about structure — bullet points, half-sentences, whatever's fast to capture live.

Step 2 — 2 min after

Summarize with AI

Highlight the raw notes and select Summarize from the AI menu — this turns rough notes into a clean, readable summary.

Step 3 — 2 min

Extract action items

Use a custom AI prompt — "extract action items with owners" — to pull a task list directly from the notes.

Step 4 — ongoing

Use AI Q&A later to recall decisions

Weeks later, when someone asks "what did we decide about X," search it via Ask AI instead of manually digging through old meeting pages.

Common Beginner Mistakes

  • Only using the AI Writer. Most of the real time savings come from Q&A and Autofill, not generating new text — those get skipped by users who never look past the write button.
  • Enabling AI on an empty workspace. Q&A has nothing to search if your workspace doesn't have real content in it yet — build up your notes and docs first.
  • Expecting web search or coding capability. Notion AI has no internet access and minimal coding ability — for anything outside your workspace, a general-purpose tool like ChatGPT is the better fit.
