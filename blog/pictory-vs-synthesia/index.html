Pictory vs Synthesia: Which AI Video Tool Should Beginners Pick?

They're both "AI video generators," but they solve different problems. Here's the difference that actually matters before you pick one.

By RankerToolAI · Updated July 12, 2026 · 6 min read

Quick Answer

Pick Pictory if you're repurposing blog posts or scripts into video with stock footage and captions. Pick Synthesia if you specifically need an AI avatar talking on camera — they aren't really competing for the same job.

The Core Difference

Pictory takes text — a blog post, a script, an article URL — and assembles it into a video using auto-matched stock footage, captions, and an optional voiceover. There's no presenter on screen; it's built for B-roll-style content repurposing.

Synthesia does the opposite: it generates a video where an AI avatar appears on camera and speaks your script directly to the viewer, in one of 140+ languages. It's built for talking-head content — training videos, corporate explainers, onboarding material — where a presenter format matters.

Pictory: Pros & Cons

Pictory Strengths

  • Cheaper starting price
  • 3 free videos to test before paying
  • Fastest way to repurpose existing blog content
  • No need to write a presenter-style script

Synthesia Strengths

  • Real AI presenter on camera, not just B-roll
  • 140+ languages, strong for global training content
  • SCORM/LMS export for corporate training platforms
  • Consistent branded presenter across every video

A Practical Example

Say you've written a 1,500-word blog post reviewing a product. Feed that post into Pictory and you'll get a video with matched b-roll, on-screen captions, and a voiceover reading the key points — ready for YouTube or a Reel in a few minutes, no camera or presenter required.

Now say you need to onboard new employees across five countries with a consistent training video. Writing a script and generating it through Synthesia with a branded avatar in each local language produces a far more professional, trustworthy result than stock footage with captions — viewers expect a presenter for training content in a way they don't for a quick social recap.

Which One Should You Actually Pick?

If your content already exists as text and you want a video version for YouTube or social fast, Pictory is the cheaper, simpler starting point — the free 3-video trial is enough to judge fit on a real post. If you need a consistent on-camera presenter for training, onboarding, or explainer content — especially across multiple languages — Synthesia is the right category of tool even though it costs more per month.

Some creators genuinely use both: Pictory for fast social repurposing of blog content, Synthesia for polished presenter-style videos where a talking head builds more trust than stock footage and captions alone.

FAQs

What's the main difference between Pictory and Synthesia?

Pictory turns existing text (blog posts, scripts) into videos using stock footage, captions, and voiceover — it doesn't use a presenter. Synthesia generates videos with an AI avatar that speaks on camera, built for talking-head presenter-style content like training and explainer videos.

Which is cheaper, Pictory or Synthesia?

Pictory, at roughly $19-20/month versus Synthesia's $29/month Starter plan. Pictory also offers 3 free videos to test before subscribing; Synthesia has a more limited free tier.

Which one should a beginner start with?

If you already have blog content and want to repurpose it into video fast, start with Pictory — it's cheaper and the workflow is simpler. If you specifically need a presenter talking on camera (training, corporate explainers), Synthesia is the right tool even though it costs more.
