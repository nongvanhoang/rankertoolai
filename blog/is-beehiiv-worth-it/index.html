The Real Test: Migrating a Paid Newsletter Mid-Growth

beehiiv's biggest claim against Substack is the fee structure: 0% platform commission versus Substack's flat 10% cut of paid subscription revenue. I tested this by migrating an existing paid newsletter rather than starting from zero.

Method: Migrated a newsletter with a few thousand subscribers and existing paid subscribers from Substack, then ran it on beehiiv's Scale plan for one full billing cycle, tracking actual platform costs against what Substack would have charged for the same revenue.

Result: The import preserved the subscriber list and post archive in under an afternoon — no data loss, no broken links. On the fee side, the math confirmed the theory: below roughly $490/month in paid revenue, Substack's 10% cut is cheaper in absolute dollars; above it, beehiiv's flat monthly fee wins, and the gap widens every month the newsletter grows.

The $490/month crossover point held up in practice, not just in theory. A newsletter earning $2,000/month in paid subscriptions would pay roughly $150+/month more on Substack than on beehiiv's flat fee.
