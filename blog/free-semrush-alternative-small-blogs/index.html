Free (and Cheap) Semrush Alternatives for Small Blogs

Semrush is genuinely excellent — and genuinely overkill for a blog with a dozen posts. Here's what to actually use for keyword research and rank tracking at a fraction of $139.95/month.

The Truly Free Option: Google Search Console

Before paying for anything, Google Search Console is free and shows exactly which queries your own site already ranks for, click-through rates, and indexing issues. For a small blog just starting out, this alone covers a meaningful chunk of what you'd otherwise pay for.

Where Search Console Falls Short

Search Console only shows data for your own site — it can't research keywords you don't rank for yet, analyze competitors, or track daily rank movement over time. That's the gap paid tools fill, and it's where Semrush's $139.95/month price tag becomes hard to justify for a small blog that just needs keyword ideas and rank tracking, not an enterprise competitive-intelligence platform.

The Real Question

Do you need Semrush's backlink database, or just keyword research and rank tracking?

Semrush's biggest advantage over cheaper tools is backlink data depth — if you're not doing serious link-building analysis yet, you're paying for a feature you won't use.

Mangools: Cheapest Full Suite

Mangools bundles five tools — KWFinder (keyword research), SERPChecker, SERPWatcher (rank tracking), LinkMiner (backlinks), and SiteProfiler — starting around $29/month on annual billing. KWFinder's keyword difficulty accuracy is well regarded even compared to tools several times the price, making it the best pick if budget is the main constraint.

SE Ranking: More Features, Still Cheap

SE Ranking starts around $65/month and covers close to the full Semrush workflow — rank tracking, keyword research, site audits (130+ technical checks), and white-label reporting — at under half the price. Rank tracking accuracy consistently lands within 1-2 positions of Google Search Console.

Which One Should You Pick?

If you're a solo blogger with a handful of posts, start with Search Console for free, then add Mangools once you need real keyword research — its 10-day trial is enough to test whether the workflow fits before paying. If you're managing client sites or need white-label reports, SE Ranking's 14-day trial is worth testing before jumping straight to Semrush's price tag.

FAQs

Is there a truly free alternative to Semrush?

Google Search Console is free and covers your own site's ranking data well, but it can't research competitor keywords. For competitor and keyword research on a budget, Mangools' 10-day trial and SE Ranking's 14-day trial are the cheapest paid entry points, starting around $29-65/month versus Semrush's $139.95/month.

Is SE Ranking as good as Semrush?

For solo bloggers and small sites, yes — SE Ranking's rank tracking matches Google Search Console closely and covers the core SEO workflow. Semrush's advantage is a much larger backlink database, which matters more for agencies and larger competitive niches.

Is Mangools good enough for a small blog?

Yes, and it's the cheapest full SEO suite of the three — KWFinder's keyword accuracy is well regarded even compared to tools 4x the price, though its backlink and site audit depth is lighter than SE Ranking or Semrush.
