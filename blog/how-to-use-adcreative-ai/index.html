What AdCreative.ai Actually Does

AdCreative.ai, now owned by Appier, generates conversion-optimized ad creatives at scale using its Creative Scoring AI — trained on 450M+ ads and $34B+ in ad spend. It produces 20+ ad variants in under 5 minutes for Facebook, Instagram, Google, and LinkedIn, each scored for predicted performance before you spend money running them.

What AdCreative.ai Does Best

Scoring designs before you test them with real ad spend

Creative Scoring combines Component Analysis (logos, CTAs, product placement, text hierarchy) with Saliency AI (visual attention prediction) — a genuine triage layer for prioritizing which variants deserve real budget.

Getting Started: Account Setup

Go to adcreative.ai and start the 7-day free trial — 10 credits, enough to generate and score a real batch of ad variants before committing to a paid plan.

Generating Ad Creative

How to Generate a Batch of Ads

  1. Click New Ad Set and select your target platform (Facebook, Instagram, Google, LinkedIn)
  2. Upload your product images and brand assets, or connect your brand's existing creative library
  3. Enter your ad copy or let AdCreative.ai suggest variations
  4. Click Generate — 20+ variants typically complete in under 5 minutes

Reading Creative Scores

How to Use the Creative Score

  1. Each generated variant displays a predicted performance score
  2. Sort your batch by score to identify the top candidates worth spending real budget on first
  3. Use the score as a prioritization signal, not an absolute guarantee — real A/B testing on your actual audience is still the final validation step
Pro Tip

Test the top 2-3 scored variants, not the entire batch

Scoring's real value is cutting the field before spending test budget — running all 20+ generated variants defeats the purpose of having a prioritization tool in the first place.

Video Ad Generation

How to Generate Video Ads

  1. Video generation requires the $249/month Professional plan or higher — confirm your plan supports it before starting
  2. Upload source footage or select from AdCreative.ai's video templates, including UGC-style formats
  3. Apply the same Creative Scoring workflow to video variants as static ads

Managing Multiple Brands

Agencies or teams running ads for multiple brands can set up separate brand profiles, each with its own asset library and creative history.

  1. Go to Brand Settings and create a new brand profile
  2. Upload that brand's logo, color palette, and asset library
  3. Switch between brand profiles when generating creative for different clients

Full Workflow: Launching a New Campaign

Here's the workflow for a new ad campaign from scratch:

Step 1 — 10 min

Upload product assets and generate a full variant batch

20+ variants across your target platform give real options to compare.

Step 2 — 5 min

Sort by Creative Score and select the top candidates

2-3 top-scored variants are usually enough to start real testing.

Step 3 — outside AdCreative.ai

Launch a small-budget test on the selected variants

Real ad platform data is the final validation the Creative Score is meant to inform, not replace.

Step 4

Scale budget toward whichever variant actually performs

Use real test results, not just the predicted score, to make the final scaling decision.

Common Beginner Mistakes

  • Testing every generated variant with real budget. The score exists to narrow the field first — test the top few, not the whole batch.
  • Assuming Starter includes video. Video generation is completely locked until the $249/month Professional plan.
  • Letting credits go unused near month-end. Credits don't roll over, so plan generation batches to use your allotment within the billing cycle.
  • Treating the Creative Score as a final answer instead of a prioritization signal. It predicts performance with 90%+ claimed accuracy, but real ad platform data on your specific audience is still the actual test — use the score to decide what to try first, not what to skip testing entirely.

Customizing Ads After Generation

Post-generation editing inside AdCreative.ai is more limited than a dedicated design tool like Canva or Figma, which affects how you should plan your workflow.

  1. Use AdCreative.ai's built-in editor for small text and color adjustments directly after generation
  2. For deeper design changes — custom layouts, brand-specific templates, complex compositions — export the top-scored variants and finish them in a dedicated design tool
  3. Re-upload finished designs if you want them included in future scoring comparisons within AdCreative.ai

Planning for this two-step handoff from the start avoids frustration later — AdCreative.ai's strength is generation speed and scoring, not full creative control, so pairing it with a design tool for the final pass gets the best of both.
