What GearUP Booster Offers

Adaptive Intelligent Routing (AIR)

GearUP Booster's core technology, which it markets as "Adaptive Intelligent Routing," routes your game traffic through its own private server network instead of your ISP's default path, aiming to find a shorter, less congested route to the game's data center. This is the same fundamental model used by every gaming network booster in the category (ExitLag and WTFast included) — the value depends entirely on whether your specific connection has a genuine routing inefficiency to route around.

Broad Game & Platform Coverage

GearUP Booster lists support for over 3,000 games, spanning major competitive titles (Valorant, League of Legends, CS2, Apex Legends, PUBG) as well as smaller or regional titles that narrower competitors sometimes skip. Coverage extends across PC, mobile (iOS/Android), and consoles including PlayStation, Xbox, Nintendo Switch, and Steam Deck — a single account can be used across whichever platforms you actually play on.

Optional HYPEREV Router Hardware

Beyond the software client, GearUP offers HYPEREV, a dedicated gaming router as a one-time hardware purchase (roughly $55.99), which applies the same routing optimization at the network level for every device on your home network, rather than requiring a client installed on each device individually. This is a differentiator from most software-only competitors, though it's an optional add-on rather than a requirement.

Trial Terms Worth Reading Before You Sign Up

GearUP Booster offers a 3-day free trial with full access — but it requires a payment method on file, and reviewers note it auto-charges the subscription price if not cancelled before the trial period ends. There's also no standard, automatic money-back guarantee published; refund requests are reportedly evaluated case-by-case rather than granted as a default policy. Neither of these is unusual for the category, but both are worth knowing going in, especially if you're only testing whether the routing actually helps your specific connection.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is GearUP Booster worth it?

It depends on whether your lag is actually routing-related. If a traceroute to your game's server shows a congested hop between your ISP and the destination — common for overseas servers or smaller regional hosts — GearUP Booster's optimized routing can genuinely help. If your ping is already low and stable on a wired connection, a booster has little room to improve on that, regardless of which one you pick.

Does GearUP Booster have a free trial?

Yes, a 3-day free trial with full access. It requires a payment method on file, and auto-charges the subscription price if not cancelled before the trial ends — set a reminder if you're only testing it.

How much does GearUP Booster cost?

PC plans run about $9.99/month, $24.99/quarter (~$8.33/mo), or $79.99/year (~$6.67/mo). Mobile pricing varies by region, generally between $4.49 and $9.99/month. There's also an optional one-time HYPEREV gaming router hardware purchase for router-level optimization.

What games and platforms does GearUP Booster support?

GearUP Booster lists support for over 3,000 games and covers PC, mobile (iOS/Android), and consoles including PlayStation, Xbox, and Nintendo Switch, plus Steam Deck. Coverage includes major competitive titles like Valorant, League of Legends, CS2, Apex Legends, and PUBG.

What are the best GearUP Booster alternatives?

The most commonly compared alternatives are ExitLag (similar routing-optimization model, comparable pricing) and WTFast (an older, more established player in the category, generally priced higher). GearUP Booster's main differentiators are its broad game list and its optional dedicated router hardware.

Does GearUP Booster offer refunds?

GearUP Booster does not publish a standard money-back guarantee; refund requests are reportedly handled case-by-case rather than as an automatic policy. This is worth factoring in before committing to a longer billing term.

Is a gaming network booster the same as a VPN?

No. A general-purpose VPN adds encryption and typically increases latency, since traffic takes a longer, encrypted path. A gaming network booster like GearUP is built specifically to reduce latency by routing traffic through an optimized path to the game server, without the encryption overhead of a full VPN.
