Our Verdict

beehiiv is the strongest newsletter platform we've tested for creators who want to grow and monetize, not just publish. The free Launch plan supports up to 2,500 subscribers with unlimited sends, a full website, a podcast page, and custom domains — genuinely usable, not a crippled trial. Once you outgrow it, Scale adds monetization (ads, paid subscriptions, boosts/referrals) with 0% platform commission, automations, and A/B testing. The pricing scales with subscriber count rather than being flat, which is fair for small lists but adds up at scale (up to ~$329/month at 100,000 subscribers on Scale). Compared to Substack, beehiiv trades a bit of simplicity for meaningfully more growth and analytics tooling.

Key Features Tested

Newsletter, Website & Podcast in One

Unlike tools that only handle email, beehiiv bundles a full website (not just a subscribe page) and a podcast hosting page into every plan, including Launch. This matters because a newsletter's website is often the main discovery surface for new readers arriving from search or social — beehiiv treats it as a first-class product rather than an afterthought.

Monetization: Ads, Paid Subscriptions & Boosts

beehiiv's ad network lets even small newsletters run sponsorships without needing to sell ads themselves. Paid subscriptions charge 0% platform commission — you only pay Stripe's standard 2.9% + $0.30 processing fee, which is meaningfully cheaper than Substack's 10% cut at any meaningful revenue. The Boosts (referral) network lets other beehiiv newsletters recommend yours and vice versa, which is a genuinely useful, low-effort growth channel that most competitors don't offer.

Analytics Beyond Opens and Clicks

Advanced analytics (Scale and up) track subscriber growth sources, engagement trends over time, and revenue attribution — not just the standard open-rate/click-rate metrics most email tools stop at. For creators trying to figure out which channels actually drive paying subscribers, this is one of beehiiv's stronger advantages.

Automations & A/B Testing

Scale unlocks automation workflows (welcome sequences, tagging based on behavior) and A/B testing for subject lines and content. These are standard in dedicated email marketing tools but less common bundled into a newsletter-first platform at this price point.

Best beehiiv Alternatives

  • Substack — Simpler to start, built-in discovery network, but takes 10% of paid subscription revenue vs. beehiiv's 0%.
  • ConvertKit — Stronger for creators who need advanced email automation and a mature creator-commerce ecosystem; less newsletter-website-first than beehiiv.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is beehiiv?

beehiiv is a newsletter platform that bundles email publishing, a full website, a podcast page, monetization tools (ads, paid subscriptions, boosts), and detailed analytics into one product, built by former Morning Brew engineers to help newsletters grow and monetize.

Does beehiiv have a free plan?

Yes. The Launch plan is free forever for up to 2,500 subscribers, with unlimited email sends, a website, a podcast page, basic analytics, custom domain support, and read-only API access. No credit card required.

How much does beehiiv cost?

Launch is free up to 2,500 subscribers. Scale starts around $49/month ($43/month billed annually) for small lists and scales with subscriber count, up to roughly $329/month at 100,000 subscribers. Max starts around $109/month ($96/month annually). Enterprise pricing is custom for 100,000+ subscribers.

Is there a beehiiv discount or free trial?

Yes — new users get a 14-day free trial of paid features with no credit card required, and through RankerToolAI's link you also get 20% off your first 3 months on any paid plan.

Does beehiiv take a cut of my subscription revenue?

No. beehiiv charges 0% platform commission on paid subscription revenue across all paid plans. You still pay Stripe's standard processing fee (2.9% + $0.30 per transaction), since Stripe handles the actual payment processing.

beehiiv vs Substack — which is better?

Substack is simpler to start with and has a built-in discovery network, but takes 10% of paid subscription revenue. beehiiv charges 0% platform commission and includes more built-in growth tools — a referral/boosts network, an ad marketplace, automations, and deeper analytics. For creators focused purely on writing, Substack's simplicity can be appealing. For creators who want to actively grow and monetize a list, beehiiv's toolset is more complete.
