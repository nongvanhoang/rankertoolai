Key Features We Tested

Text-Based Editing

The core Descript workflow: upload audio or video, wait for transcription (usually 2-5 minutes for a 1-hour file), then edit the transcript like a document. Delete a word in the transcript and the corresponding audio is removed. Highlight a paragraph and delete it, and the whole segment is cut. This sounds simple but is genuinely transformative in practice.

For our test episode — a 45-minute podcast interview — removing all "um," "uh," and filler words took 3 minutes using the automatic "Remove filler words" feature. The same task in Audition would take 20-30 minutes of careful timeline scrubbing. Rearranging interview segments by cutting and pasting paragraphs in the transcript took 15 minutes. In traditional editing, this would have been a 45-minute job.

AI Transcription Quality

Descript's transcription is among the best we've tested, consistently reaching 95-97% accuracy on clean recordings with one or two speakers. Speaker identification is automatic and accurate — it correctly labeled 4 different speakers in our roundtable test with only one mis-labeling across a 90-minute recording.

Accuracy drops on technical jargon (AI tool names, software terms), strong accents, and overlapping speech. For a typical podcast or interview, accuracy is high enough that the transcript-based workflow is significantly faster than traditional editing even when accounting for correction time. Versus Otter.ai, transcription quality is comparable; Descript's advantage is that the transcript is directly connected to the audio for editing, not just a separate document.

Overdub Voice Cloning

Overdub lets you type new words or sentences and have them rendered in your cloned voice. It's designed for fixing errors — mispronounced names, forgotten transitions, awkward phrasing — without re-recording. After training a voice clone with 30 minutes of audio, our test Overdub clips were 80% convincing on first pass. Longer, more unnatural sentences showed slight artifacts, but short fixes for words and sentences were nearly seamless.

The ethical safeguards are robust: Descript requires voice consent verification before creating a clone, and watermarks all Overdub audio at the file level. For podcast producers, Overdub is a genuine time-saver that eliminates most re-recording sessions.

Video Editing (Storyboard)

Descript's video editing via the Storyboard interface follows the same text-editing paradigm. You can cut video segments by deleting transcript text, add B-roll, insert text overlays, and apply basic transitions. For YouTube videos up to 10 minutes, this workflow works well. For longer, more complex productions — interviews with multiple camera angles, cinematic grade color, complex motion graphics — you'll need a traditional NLE like Premiere or Final Cut.

A particularly useful feature: Descript can generate a transcript-based summary and chapter markers for YouTube automatically. This alone saves significant post-production time for video creators.

AI Features: Studio Sound & Eye Contact

Studio Sound is Descript's AI noise removal and audio enhancement feature. Applied to a recording made in a moderately noisy environment, it reduced background noise by approximately 80% and added mild presence boost. Not as good as iZotope RX for complex noise removal, but adequate for most podcast recordings and significantly faster.

Eye Contact correction automatically adjusts your gaze direction to appear to be looking directly at the camera, even when you're reading notes. In our tests, it worked convincingly at normal viewing distances but showed slight artifacts on close inspection. Useful for talking-head video creators.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Descript used for?

Descript is used for podcast editing, video editing, and audio production. Its core innovation is text-based editing — you edit audio and video by editing the auto-generated transcript, which is far faster than traditional timeline editing for most content creator workflows.

How accurate is Descript's transcription?

Descript's AI transcription accuracy is approximately 95-97% for clear English speech in good audio conditions. It handles multiple speakers well and automatically labels different voices. For technical jargon or accented speech, accuracy drops slightly but remains usable.

What is Descript Overdub?

Descript Overdub is an AI voice cloning feature that creates a synthetic copy of your voice. You can use it to fix mispronounced words, fill in forgotten lines, or generate entirely new sentences — all in your own voice without re-recording. It requires 30+ minutes of training audio and takes 24-48 hours to train.

Does Descript work for video editing?

Yes. Descript handles video editing through its Storyboard feature, which lets you edit video by rearranging text in a transcript. You can add B-roll, text overlays, and transitions. It's not a replacement for Adobe Premiere, but for simple to moderate video editing tasks, it's significantly faster.

How much does Descript cost?

Descript costs $0 (Free, 1hr transcription/month), $24/month (Hobbyist, 10hr/month), $40/month (Creator, 30hr/month + 4K export), and custom Business pricing. Annual billing saves 25%. The Hobbyist plan is enough for most solo podcasters.

Is Descript better than Audacity for podcasting?

For speed and ease of use, Descript is significantly better than Audacity for most podcasters. Descript's text-based editing, automatic filler word removal, and Overdub fix common podcast editing tasks far faster. Audacity remains superior for precise audio engineering — compression, EQ, noise gating with fine control. Most podcasters should use Descript; audio engineers should use both.
