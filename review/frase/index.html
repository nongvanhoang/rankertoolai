What Is Frase?

Frase is an AI-powered content research and optimization platform built specifically for SEO writers. Unlike general AI writers (Jasper, Writesonic), Frase starts from the SERP — it scrapes Google's top 20 results for your target keyword and distills them into a structured content brief: key headings, statistics, frequently asked questions, and topic coverage gaps.

The core workflow: enter a keyword → Frase builds a brief → you (or Frase's AI writer) fill in the sections → Frase scores your content against the SERP competitors. It's a research-first tool, not a raw content generator.

Content Brief Generation: Where Frase Shines

This is Frase's killer feature. Enter "best CRM software 2026" and within 90 seconds you have: the average word count of top-ranking articles, the most common H2/H3 headings, all People Also Ask questions, key statistics cited across sources, and a content score baseline. Tasks that would take a human researcher 2-3 hours take Frase under 2 minutes.

The brief export to Google Docs or PDF is clean — useful for agencies handing briefs to freelance writers. Team collaboration features let multiple editors work on the same document simultaneously.

Frase vs Surfer SEO: The Real Comparison

These two tools are frequently compared but serve slightly different primary use cases. Frase excels at the research and briefing phase — before writing starts. Surfer SEO excels during writing — real-time NLP scoring as you type. Many high-output content teams actually use both: Frase to build the brief, Surfer to optimize as they write.

If forced to choose one: if your bottleneck is research and brief quality, choose Frase. If your bottleneck is content ranking despite being well-researched, choose Surfer SEO.

Not ideal for: Solo bloggers on tight budgets (the Solo plan's 4 articles/month limit is restrictive), teams already satisfied with Surfer SEO's research features, or users who primarily need an AI writing assistant.

Real-World Workflow: How Content Teams Use Frase

After testing Frase across 3 months of real content production, here is the workflow that consistently delivers the best results for SEO-focused bloggers and content teams.

Step 1 — Brief Generation (5 minutes): Enter your target keyword and let Frase scrape the top 20 SERP results. Review the auto-generated headings, identify which questions competitors answer, and note the average word count across top-ranking pages. Tasks that would take a human researcher 2–3 hours are done in under 5 minutes. The SERP data Frase pulls is reliably current — it reflects what is ranking today, not cached data from months ago.

Step 2 — Outline Customization (10 minutes): Drag and drop headings from competitor pages into your brief structure. Add your unique angle — your own data, counter-arguments, or specific expertise the top results lack. The brief becomes your article's working skeleton. Teams can share this brief directly with freelance writers as a Google Doc or PDF export, removing the back-and-forth of brief clarification.

Step 3 — Content Optimization (ongoing as you write): As you draft, Frase's content score tracks which topics you have covered and which are still missing. Aim for a score above 65 for competitive keywords. The optimization panel highlights exact terms and phrases from top-ranking pages that your draft should include to compete on topic coverage.

Best Pairing: Use Frase for brief creation and research, then paste your outline into Surfer SEO's Content Editor for real-time NLP scoring as you write. This two-tool stack — roughly $200/month combined — replaces a full-time content researcher and speeds up time-to-publish on well-optimized articles significantly. Teams already using Surfer who add Frase report cutting total research-to-publish time by 40%.
