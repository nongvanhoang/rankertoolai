Our Verdict

Clearscope earns its premium price through term-report accuracy and an interface writers actually enjoy using. The A–F content grade and inline term tracking make it the easiest content-optimization tool to hand to a non-SEO writer and get good results immediately. It doesn't include content briefs or topic-cluster planning the way MarketMuse does, and at $170/month minimum it's priced for agencies and serious content teams, not solo bloggers. If your content team ships 10+ articles a month and needs writers to self-optimize without SEO hand-holding, Clearscope is worth every dollar. If you're a solo creator on a budget, Surfer SEO or Frase deliver 80% of the value for less.

Clearscope Features — Tested

Term Reports — The Core Engine

Clearscope analyzes the top-ranking pages for your target keyword and returns a list of terms (with target usage counts) that correlate with ranking. In our testing, the term suggestions consistently matched what we'd find by manually auditing top-10 SERPs — but in seconds instead of hours. Terms are color-coded by importance, so writers know which ones move the needle most.

Content Grade (A–F)

As you write inside the Google Docs add-on, Clearscope scores your draft in real time on an A–F scale based on term usage, word count, and readability. This single number is the reason non-SEO writers can use Clearscope without training — hit a B+ or higher and you're competitive.

Content Inventory

Connect your site and Clearscope audits every published page, flagging which ones are losing rankings and need a refresh. This turns Clearscope from a pre-publish tool into an ongoing content maintenance system — genuinely useful for sites with 100+ articles.

Compare & Competitor Analysis

The Compare tab lets you benchmark your draft directly against the current top 10 ranking pages for a keyword, term-by-term. It's the fastest way to see exactly what a competitor covers that you don't.

Google Docs & WordPress Integration

Unlike tools that force you into a proprietary editor, Clearscope's add-on overlays scoring directly inside Google Docs and Microsoft Word. The WordPress plugin does the same inside the block editor. This is consistently the most-praised UX detail among content teams we surveyed.

Best Clearscope Alternatives

Clearscope isn't for everyone. Here are the top alternatives depending on your needs:

  • Surfer SEO — Similar term-report approach at a much lower entry price (~$89/mo). Best budget alternative.
  • MarketMuse — Adds content briefs and topic-cluster planning. Better for content strategy, not just optimization.
  • Frase — Combines content briefs with AI writing at a fraction of the cost (~$45/mo).
  • SEO writing checkers in Semrush/SE Ranking — If you already pay for a full SEO suite, the built-in content tools may be enough without a dedicated subscription.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Clearscope worth it?

Clearscope is worth it for content teams publishing 10+ optimized articles per month who need writers to self-optimize without deep SEO knowledge. At $170/month minimum, it's overkill for solo bloggers — Surfer SEO or Frase deliver similar core functionality for less.

Does Clearscope have a free trial?

Clearscope does not offer a self-serve free trial. You need to book a demo with their sales team, who can set up a limited trial period depending on your use case.

How much does Clearscope cost?

Clearscope's Essentials plan starts at $170/month for 20 content reports. Business and Enterprise tiers require custom quotes and typically start around $1,000+/month for teams needing unlimited reports and multiple seats.

Clearscope vs Surfer SEO — which is better?

Clearscope has more accurate term reports and a more polished writer experience, but Surfer SEO is roughly 50% cheaper and includes an AI writing assistant. For agencies with budget, Clearscope; for solo creators and small teams, Surfer SEO is the better value.

Does Clearscope use AI to write content?

No. Clearscope is a content grading and optimization tool, not a content generator. It analyzes and scores content you write — it doesn't draft it for you. Pair it with an AI writer like Jasper or Writesonic if you need both.

What is Clearscope's Content Grade?

The Content Grade is an A–F score calculated from term usage, term count, and readability compared to top-ranking pages for your target keyword. It updates live as you write inside the Google Docs add-on, making it easy to hit a publishable grade before you finish a draft.

Is Clearscope good for agencies?

Yes — Clearscope's Business and Enterprise tiers support multiple users and unlimited reports, and the Content Inventory feature lets agencies monitor and refresh client content at scale. It's one of the most agency-friendly content optimization tools available, provided the budget supports it.
