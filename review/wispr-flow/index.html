Our Verdict

Wispr Flow is one of the most polished AI voice dictation tools we've tested. Speak naturally — including "ums," restarts, and rambling — and its AI Auto Edits layer turns it into clean, correctly formatted text inside whatever app you're using, from Gmail to VS Code. Setup takes under two minutes on Mac or Windows, and the personal dictionary quickly learns names and jargon it initially mishears. The free plan (2,000 words/week) is generous enough to properly evaluate it, and Pro at $12/month (billed annually) is reasonably priced against typing-productivity tools in this category. The main limitations: the free tier's weekly cap will feel tight if you dictate daily, and accuracy — like any speech tool — depends on your microphone and how noisy your environment is.

Key Features Tested

System-Wide Voice Dictation

Wispr Flow runs in the background on Mac and Windows and activates in whatever text field has focus — Gmail, Slack, Notion, Google Docs, Cursor, VS Code, or a browser form. There's no separate "recording window" to manage: you hit the shortcut, talk, and the text appears where your cursor is. This is the single biggest differentiator versus dictation built into the OS, which tends to be clunkier and less accurate at punctuation.

AI Auto Edits

Raw speech is messy — filler words, false starts, run-on sentences. Wispr Flow's AI Auto Edits layer cleans this up in real time, removing "um"/"like," fixing grammar, and adding punctuation and paragraph breaks that match how you'd actually write the thought. In our testing, dictated emails and Slack messages needed little to no manual cleanup afterward — a clear step up from raw speech-to-text transcription tools.

Personal Dictionary & Snippets

The personal dictionary learns names, acronyms, and jargon that generic speech models usually mishear — useful for technical teams, medical/legal terminology, or non-English names. The Snippet Library lets you set up voice shortcuts for boilerplate text (email signatures, standard responses, code comments), which cut noticeably into repetitive typing during our testing.

Command Mode (Pro)

Command Mode, available on Pro, lets you edit text by voice — select, delete, rephrase, or reformat without touching the keyboard. It's the feature that pushes Wispr Flow from "dictation tool" toward "voice-first writing workflow," and it's the main reason to upgrade past the free plan if you dictate daily.

Speed & Accuracy

Per Wispr Flow's own benchmarks, dictation runs at roughly 220 words per minute versus about 45 wpm for average typing — a claim consistent with what we observed for sustained dictation of straightforward prose. Accuracy was strong in quiet rooms with a decent microphone; it degrades with background noise, heavy accents the model hasn't adapted to yet, or very unusual proper nouns before you've trained the dictionary.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Wispr Flow?

Wispr Flow is an AI voice dictation app that converts spoken words into clean, formatted text in any application on Mac, Windows, iPhone, or Android — Gmail, Slack, Docs, Notion, VS Code, and more. Its AI Auto Edits feature removes filler words and fixes grammar in real time.

Does Wispr Flow have a free plan?

Yes. The free Basic plan includes 2,000 words/week on Mac or Windows, 1,000 words/week on iPhone, and unlimited words/week on Android (for a limited time), plus the custom dictionary, snippets, and 100+ languages. Every new account also gets a 14-day Flow Pro trial with no credit card required.

How much does Wispr Flow cost?

Pro costs $15/month billed monthly, or $12/month billed annually ($144/year). Team plans cost $12/user/month monthly or $10/user/month annually (3-seat minimum). Enterprise pricing is custom.

Is there a Wispr Flow coupon code?

We could not verify any working third-party discount code. The reliable ways to pay less are billing annually (20% off Pro), using the 14-day free Pro trial first, or applying to the Flow Discounts program if you qualify as a student, educator, non-profit, military member, senior, or have an accessibility need.

What platforms does Wispr Flow support?

macOS, Windows, iPhone, and Android — and it works system-wide, typing into any text field rather than a dedicated app window. Your personal dictionary and snippets sync across devices.

How accurate is Wispr Flow?

Accuracy is strong for clear speech in quiet environments, and AI Auto Edits improves the readable output further by removing filler words and fixing grammar. Accuracy drops with background noise, low-quality microphones, or uncommon names — though the personal dictionary largely resolves the jargon problem after a few corrections.

Is Wispr Flow worth it compared to typing?

For people who write a lot of email, chat, or documentation, yes — dictation runs at roughly 220 wpm versus about 45 wpm for average typing, per Wispr Flow's own benchmarks. The free plan's 2,000 words/week is enough to judge the fit before paying. It's less useful for people who mainly write code or need silent input in shared spaces.
