Our Verdict

AirOps is a genuinely capable content operations platform for teams that already think in systems and workflows — its drag-and-drop builder chains keyword research, competitor scraping, AI drafting, internal linking, and publishing into pipelines that run at a scale a single AI writing tool can't match, and the Grids content calendar makes managing hundreds of pieces from one view feel manageable rather than chaotic. The AI search visibility tracking (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews) is a genuinely useful addition as more traffic shifts to AI answer engines. The tradeoffs are real: the learning curve is steep — this isn't a tool you pick up in an afternoon — and task-based pricing makes monthly cost hard to forecast until you've built a usage history. Solo creators or teams that just want fast AI drafts are better served by a simpler tool; AirOps earns its price for teams with real content volume and process maturity to invest in setup.

Key Features Tested

No-Code Workflow Builder

AirOps' core is a drag-and-drop workflow builder that chains steps — a Google search for keyword research, competitor page scraping, AI-driven content drafting, internal linking, meta tag generation — and runs them in sequence or parallel. Instead of stitching together separate tools for research, writing, and optimization, you build one repeatable pipeline once and reuse it across every new piece of content.

Grids — Spreadsheet-Style Content Ops

Grids is AirOps' take on a content calendar where each row is a content piece and each column is a process stage — research, draft, review, publish — and you can run AI workflows directly from the grid. In our testing, this is the feature that most changes how a team actually works day to day: reviewing and publishing hundreds of pieces from one spreadsheet-style interface, rather than jumping between docs and a CMS.

AI Search Visibility Tracking

Beyond content production, AirOps tracks how your brand shows up across AI answer engines — Page360, Sentiment Tracking, and Query Fan-outs surface visibility data alongside standard GA4 and Google Search Console signals. As more search traffic shifts to ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews, this is a genuinely forward-looking addition that most pure content-drafting tools don't offer.

Integrations & Model Access

Even the free plan includes access to 30+ AI models and 10+ data providers, plus 10+ CMS integrations, so you're not locked into a single model vendor. Paid plans add SEO, AEO (answer engine optimization), social, and project-management integrations, letting workflows publish directly into the tools a content team already uses.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is AirOps?

AirOps is a no-code AI content operations platform that connects data sources, AI models, and CMS platforms into automated workflows. Its drag-and-drop builder chains keyword research, competitor scraping, AI drafting, internal linking, and meta tag generation, and its Grids feature runs those workflows directly from a spreadsheet-style content calendar.

Does AirOps have a free plan?

Yes. The free Insights plan includes 1,000 tasks/month, 1 user, basic templates, 1 Brand Kit, 5 Knowledge Base sources, access to 30+ AI models and 10+ data providers, and 10+ CMS integrations. AirOps also offers a 14-day trial of full Scale/Pro features with no payment required upfront.

How much does AirOps cost?

Beyond the free tier, third-party reporting puts the Solo plan around $199/month (roughly 20,000 tasks) and Pro around $1,999/month (roughly 75,000 tasks), both with overage fees for extra usage. Enterprise pricing is custom — confirm current numbers directly with AirOps before committing.

What is AirOps best for?

AirOps fits SEO managers handling large content refresh backlogs, content teams with established editorial workflows scaling output without losing brand voice, and agencies running repeatable SEO processes across multiple clients. It's a weaker fit for solo creators who just want a simple AI writing assistant.

Is AirOps hard to learn?

Yes, more than most AI content tools. A steep learning curve is one of the most consistently repeated criticisms in user reviews — the workflow builder is powerful but requires upfront setup investment before it pays off, unlike single-purpose AI writing tools you can use in minutes.

AirOps vs a simple AI writing tool — which should I use?

If you just need help drafting individual articles, a focused AI writing tool like Jasper or Copy.ai will get you there faster with less setup. AirOps is the better choice if your bottleneck is coordinating research, drafting, internal linking, and publishing across dozens or hundreds of pages, and you're willing to invest time building repeatable workflows.
