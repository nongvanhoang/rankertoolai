Keyword Research: Industry-Leading

Semrush's keyword database contains 25+ billion keywords — the largest of any SEO tool. Keyword Magic Tool surfaces related keywords, question keywords, and semantic clusters instantly. The Keyword Gap feature compares your keyword coverage against up to 4 competitors and identifies content opportunities you're missing. In our 30-day test, we found 140+ new keyword opportunities that we hadn't identified manually.

Backlink Analysis

Semrush's backlink index updates daily and covers 43 trillion backlinks. The Backlink Audit tool identifies toxic links that could trigger Google penalties. Backlink Gap shows which sites link to competitors but not to you — a direct roadmap for link-building outreach. Compared to Ahrefs, Semrush's backlink database is slightly smaller but provides better toxicity scoring and outreach integration.

ContentShake AI: The AI Writing Feature

ContentShake AI generates article drafts informed by real SERP data for your target keyword — not just generic AI content. It analyzes the top-10 ranking pages, identifies the topics they cover, and builds a content brief automatically. The drafts include the right semantic keywords and header structure to rank. This is meaningfully better than using ChatGPT for SEO content because it's grounded in actual search data.

Semrush vs Ahrefs: Which Should You Choose?

Both are the best SEO tools available. Semrush has more tools (55+ vs Ahrefs' ~20), better keyword data volume, and superior content marketing features. Ahrefs has a cleaner UI, a stronger backlink index, and is preferred by technical SEOs who want depth over breadth. For content teams and agencies, Semrush is usually the better fit. For link builders and technical SEOs, Ahrefs is often preferred.

Who Should Use Semrush?

Semrush is the right tool for SEO professionals, digital marketing agencies, and content teams who need one platform to handle every aspect of search marketing. If you're managing 3+ client sites, publishing 10+ articles per month, or running paid search campaigns alongside organic SEO, Semrush's breadth pays for itself quickly.

Semrush is best for:

  • SEO agencies managing multiple client accounts — the white-label reporting and project management tools are unmatched
  • Content marketing teams who need keyword research, competitor gap analysis, and content optimization in one place
  • PPC marketers running Google Ads alongside SEO — Semrush's Ad Research tool shows competitor spend and ad copy
  • E-commerce businesses that need local SEO, position tracking, and product listing research
  • In-house marketing teams at mid-to-large companies that need auditable, shareable SEO data

Semrush may not be worth it if: You're a solo blogger publishing fewer than 4 articles per month, or you only need backlink research (Ahrefs is more affordable for that single use case). At $139.95/month, solo creators should consider whether Surfer SEO ($89/month) or a combination of free tools covers their needs.

Semrush Key Features Deep Dive

Site Audit

Semrush's Site Audit crawls up to 100,000 pages (Pro plan) and identifies technical SEO issues organized by severity: errors, warnings, and notices. It checks for broken links, duplicate content, slow pages, missing meta tags, hreflang errors, and 130+ other technical factors. The audit integrates with Google Analytics and Search Console to correlate technical issues with traffic drops — a feature no other tool matches at this price point.

Position Tracking

Track your keyword rankings daily across desktop and mobile, in any location down to city level. The Position Tracking dashboard shows rank changes, estimated traffic, and SERP feature ownership (featured snippets, local pack). You can track competitors' positions on the same keywords side by side — useful for identifying when a competitor steals a featured snippet you've been holding.

Link Building Tool

Semrush's Link Building tool generates a prioritized outreach list based on your target keywords and competitors' backlink profiles, integrates with Gmail for outreach, and tracks the status of each link prospect from "In progress" to "Won." It's a complete link building workflow inside one platform — no separate outreach tool needed for most teams.

In-Depth Analysis: Is Semrush Worth It?

After four years of testing Semrush across multiple client accounts and personal projects, it remains the most comprehensive SEO platform available. No single competitor matches its combination of keyword research depth, backlink analysis, technical audit quality, and competitor intelligence in one subscription. The 2026 AI features — particularly the AI-powered content brief generator and keyword clustering — have meaningfully reduced the time from research to content creation. For marketing teams or agencies managing multiple sites, the Guru plan ($250/month) is the sweet spot: unlimited keyword research, historical data, and content tools at a price that's easily justified by the time savings alone.

Who should avoid Semrush: Individuals with a single personal blog who don't need the competitive intelligence features. For solo bloggers on a budget, Ubersuggest ($12/month) or even Google Search Console plus free keyword tools covers the essentials. Semrush's value scales with the number of sites you manage and your need for competitor analysis. At one site and limited content output, the ROI case is harder to make.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does Semrush cost?

Semrush offers three main plans: Pro ($130/month) for freelancers and startups — 5 projects, 500 tracked keywords, 10,000 results per report. Guru ($250/month) for agencies and growing businesses — 15 projects, 1,500 keywords, historical data, content marketing tools. Business ($500/month) for large agencies — 40 projects, 5,000 keywords, API access. Annual billing saves approximately 17%.

Is Semrush worth the price?

For professional SEOs, agencies, and marketing teams where organic traffic drives revenue, Semrush is worth the investment. The combination of keyword research, competitive intelligence, backlink analysis, and technical auditing in one platform replaces multiple cheaper tools. For individual bloggers or small businesses with limited SEO budgets, the Pro plan ($130/month) is a significant expense — free alternatives (Google Search Console + Ubersuggest) cover the basics.

What does Semrush do?

Semrush is an all-in-one SEO and competitive intelligence platform. Key features: keyword research and difficulty analysis, competitor keyword gap analysis, backlink analysis and monitoring, technical SEO site auditing, rank tracking for your target keywords, on-page SEO recommendations, content optimization tools, local SEO management, and social media monitoring. New AI features in 2026 include AI-powered content briefs and keyword clustering.

Is Semrush better than Ahrefs?

Semrush and Ahrefs are the two leading SEO platforms — both are excellent, with different strengths. Semrush has better keyword research data volume and more comprehensive marketing features (PPC, social, content). Ahrefs has a better backlink index and more intuitive link analysis. For content marketers and agencies, Semrush's broader toolset is often preferred. For link builders and technical SEOs, many prefer Ahrefs. Most serious SEO teams have tried both.
