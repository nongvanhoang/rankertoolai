Is Grammarly Worth It?

Grammarly's free plan alone outperforms most competitors' paid tiers for basic grammar checking. Worth upgrading to Premium if you write professionally and want advanced clarity/engagement suggestions and full plagiarism detection; casual writers and students can comfortably stay on the free plan indefinitely.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Grammarly's free plan actually good enough?

Yes, for casual use. It catches grammar, spelling, and basic punctuation errors accurately and includes tone detection and 100 free Grammarly Go AI prompts per month — genuinely useful without ever paying.

What does Grammarly Premium add over the free plan?

Premium adds advanced clarity and engagement suggestions, full-document tone rewrites, a plagiarism checker, and unlimited generative AI rewriting — worthwhile for professional writers and marketers.

How much does Grammarly Premium cost?

$12/month billed annually, or $30/month billed monthly. Grammarly Business starts at $15/member/month (annual) for teams of 3 or more.

Does Grammarly work outside the browser?

Yes. Beyond the browser extension, Grammarly has native Mac and Windows desktop apps, a Microsoft Office add-in, and Slack/Teams integrations, in addition to Google Docs support.
