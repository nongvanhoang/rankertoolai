Quick Verdict

BOTTOM LINE

Semrush wins on data depth, integrations, and overall feature breadth. SE Ranking wins on price — it delivers 80% of Semrush's core functionality at 40% of the cost. Choose Semrush if you manage clients, do heavy competitor research, or need the deepest keyword database. Choose SE Ranking if you are a solo operator, small team, or startup watching costs without wanting to give up a proper rank tracker + keyword tool + site auditor.

Overview: Semrush vs SE Ranking

Semrush has been the dominant all-in-one SEO platform since 2008. It covers keyword research, competitor analysis, backlinks, rank tracking, site auditing, content marketing, local SEO, and PPC in one tool. The tradeoff: it is expensive, starting at $139.95/month.

SE Ranking launched in 2013 and has carved out a strong position as the premium-but-affordable Semrush alternative. It covers all the core SEO bases — keyword research, rank tracking, competitor analysis, backlink audit, and site audit — with a cleaner UI and substantially lower pricing starting at $52/month.

Pricing Comparison

Semrush pricing

  • Pro — $139.95/month (1 user, 5 projects, 500 keywords tracked)
  • Guru — $249.95/month (3 users, 15 projects, 1,500 keywords, content marketing platform)
  • Business — $499.95/month (5 users, 40 projects, 5,000 keywords, API access)

Annual billing saves about 17%. White-label reporting is a paid add-on on all plans.

SE Ranking pricing

  • Essential — $52/month (1 manager, 10 projects, 500 keywords)
  • Pro — $95.20/month (3 managers, unlimited projects, 2,000 keywords)
  • Business — $207.20/month (5 managers, unlimited projects, 5,000 keywords)

Annual billing saves 20%. White-label reports are included from the Essential plan. The pricing structure is also more flexible — you can add extra keyword checks and users without jumping to a higher tier.

Price gap reality: Semrush Pro at $139.95/mo vs SE Ranking Essential at $52/mo is a $88/month difference — that is $1,056 per year. For solo operators and small teams, that difference is significant. SE Ranking's 14-day trial (vs Semrush's 7-day) also gives you more time to evaluate.

One more thing to consider: Semrush's annual billing lock-in. If you commit to annual pricing and your needs change, you are locked in for 12 months. SE Ranking offers more flexibility — monthly billing is available at a smaller premium, and the tool is easier to pause or downgrade if you have a slow season. For freelancers and small agencies with variable workloads, that flexibility has real value.

Keyword Research

Semrush has the largest keyword database in the industry — 25.5 billion keywords across 142 countries. The Keyword Magic Tool lets you filter by intent, question type, SERP features, and keyword difficulty with very high accuracy. For competitive niches where keyword data precision matters, Semrush has a clear edge.

Keyword difficulty scores in Semrush are well-calibrated against real ranking difficulty. The "Keyword Gap" tool for comparing your keywords vs competitors is one of the best in the industry.

SE Ranking has a database of 5+ billion keywords — smaller, but still comprehensive for most use cases. Its Keyword Research tool provides search volume, difficulty, CPC, SERP history, and related keywords. The interface is arguably cleaner and easier to navigate than Semrush's, especially for users who are not power users.

For most keywords in mainstream niches, both tools will surface the same core opportunities. The difference shows up in very niche topics, long-tail data, and non-English markets where Semrush's larger database wins.

Rank Tracking

This is where SE Ranking genuinely competes at the same level as Semrush. SE Ranking's rank tracker is accurate, updates daily, supports local rank tracking by city/zip, tracks desktop and mobile separately, and generates clean automated reports you can white-label for clients.

Semrush's Position Tracking is equally solid — daily updates, local tracking, SERP feature tracking, competitor monitoring. Both tools support tracking across Google, Bing, Yahoo, and YouTube.

If rank tracking is your primary use case, SE Ranking gives you the same accuracy at less than half the price. This is the strongest argument for SE Ranking over Semrush.

Backlink Analysis

Semrush wins clearly here. With 43 trillion backlinks in its index, Semrush has one of the most comprehensive backlink databases available — competitive with Ahrefs. The Backlink Analytics tool shows referring domains, anchor text distribution, authority scores, link acquisition over time, and toxic link identification.

SE Ranking has a solid backlink database (3 trillion links) but it is notably smaller. For most small sites, the SE Ranking backlink data is sufficient — you will find your important links and your competitors' main link sources. For competitive analysis in high-authority niches, or if you are doing serious link building outreach, Semrush's data depth wins.

Both tools include a Backlink Audit feature for identifying and disavowing toxic links.

Site Audit

Both tools offer excellent site audit functionality and this is genuinely close to a tie in practice.

Semrush Site Audit checks 140+ technical SEO issues, generates a health score, prioritizes fixes by impact, and integrates with Google Search Console. It handles JavaScript rendering reasonably well and crawls large sites efficiently.

SE Ranking Site Audit checks 120+ issues with similar categorization (critical/warnings/notices), generates a similar health score, and also integrates with Search Console. For most sites, the SE Ranking audit surfaces the same important issues as Semrush.

The practical difference: Semrush's audit is slightly better for large enterprise sites and has more advanced JavaScript crawling. SE Ranking's audit is excellent for sites up to ~100,000 pages and is easier to set up and schedule for automated reports.

Content Marketing Features

This is a significant differentiator in Semrush's favor. Semrush's content marketing toolkit includes the SEO Content Template, SEO Writing Assistant (Chrome extension + Google Docs integration), Topic Research tool, Content Audit, and Brand Monitoring.

SE Ranking has basic content tools — a Content Marketing module with topic research and an AI content writer — but they are significantly less developed than Semrush's. If you need content optimization tools baked into your SEO suite, Semrush is the better choice (though for dedicated content optimization, Surfer SEO at $89/mo is more purpose-built).

Data Coverage

Semrush supports 142 countries. SE Ranking supports 100+ countries. For English-language markets — US, UK, Australia, Canada — both tools have strong data. For smaller markets (Eastern Europe, Southeast Asia, niche regions) Semrush has noticeably better coverage.

Semrush also has stronger PPC data — competitor ad research, Google Shopping analysis, display advertising data. If PPC management is part of your workflow, Semrush has no equivalent in SE Ranking's feature set.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is SE Ranking as good as Semrush?

SE Ranking is as good as Semrush for rank tracking and site audits — the two areas most SEOs use daily. Where Semrush wins decisively is keyword database size, backlink data depth, competitor intelligence, content tools, and international coverage. If you need those capabilities regularly, Semrush is worth the premium. If you do not, SE Ranking saves you $1,000+/year for equivalent results.

Can SE Ranking replace Semrush?

For solo operators, bloggers, and small agencies focused on ranking and auditing, yes. SE Ranking covers keyword research, rank tracking, backlinks, and site audit. What it cannot replace: Semrush's 25 billion keyword database, 43 trillion backlink index, PPC tools, and content marketing suite. If you actively use those features, the switch will hurt. If you rarely use them, SE Ranking delivers the same core value at 40% of the cost.

Which tool has better rank tracking?

SE Ranking's rank tracker matches Semrush in accuracy and features — both offer daily updates, local tracking, mobile/desktop split, and automated reports. SE Ranking includes white-label reporting at no extra cost, while Semrush charges extra. For rank tracking specifically, SE Ranking is the better value.

Does SE Ranking have a free trial?

Yes. SE Ranking offers a 14-day free trial with no credit card required. Semrush offers a 7-day free trial. SE Ranking's longer trial is a meaningful advantage when evaluating whether a tool fits your workflow.

What is the difference in keyword database size?

Semrush has approximately 25.5 billion keywords; SE Ranking has 5+ billion. In practice, for mainstream English-language niches, the difference is minimal — both surface the same key opportunities. The gap shows up in very niche topics, long-tail queries with low volume, and non-English markets. If you do research in niche or international markets, Semrush's larger database is worth the premium.

Which is better for agencies?

For large agencies managing 20+ clients, Semrush's Business plan at $499.95/month makes more sense — unlimited projects, API access, and the deepest data. For small agencies managing 5–15 clients, SE Ranking Pro at $95.20/month with white-label reports included is usually sufficient and saves over $1,800/year compared to Semrush Guru.
