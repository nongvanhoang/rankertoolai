🏆 Winner: Wispr Flow (for most daily writers)

Wispr Flow's AI Auto Edits and Command Mode produce output that needs less manual cleanup, which matters most if you dictate daily. Superwhisper wins decisively on price and privacy — its lifetime license and local processing are genuinely compelling if those matter more to you than polish. Neither tool is a bad choice; the right pick depends entirely on which of those two priorities matters more for how you actually work.

Choose Wispr Flow if you…

  • Want the least manual cleanup after dictating
  • Dictate emails, Slack, and docs daily
  • Want voice-based editing via Command Mode
  • Don't mind a monthly subscription

Choose Superwhisper if you…

  • Want the cheapest long-term option (lifetime license)
  • Need offline/local processing for privacy
  • Want a genuinely unlimited free tier
  • Use Windows as your primary platform (Superwhisper's Windows support is more mature)

Pricing: Superwhisper Wins Decisively

Superwhisper Pro starts at $8.49/month, drops to $7.08/month billed annually ($84.99/year), or a one-time $249.99 lifetime license — an option Wispr Flow doesn't offer at all. Wispr Flow Pro costs $15/month billed monthly or $12/month billed annually ($144/year), with no lifetime purchase path. Both companies also offer student discounts, though Wispr Flow's is more generous (three months free plus 50% off afterward through its Flow Discounts program).

Over three years of steady use, Superwhisper's lifetime license ($249.99 once) works out far cheaper than Wispr Flow's ongoing subscription (roughly $432 over three years at the annual rate). If you're confident you'll be dictating for years, Superwhisper's lifetime option is hard to beat on pure economics.

That said, price isn't the only variable worth modeling. Wispr Flow's team plan ($10/user/month annually, 3-seat minimum) includes centralized billing and admin controls that Superwhisper doesn't offer in the same way, which matters if you're rolling dictation out across a small team rather than buying individual licenses one at a time. For a solo user, though, the lifetime license math clearly favors Superwhisper over any multi-year subscription commitment.

AI Cleanup & Editing

Wispr Flow's AI Auto Edits layer removes filler words, fixes grammar, and adds punctuation in real time — dictated emails and Slack messages needed little to no manual cleanup in our testing. Command Mode, on Pro, lets you edit text by voice entirely: select, delete, rephrase, or reformat without touching the keyboard, which is the single biggest reason heavy daily dictators tend to prefer Wispr Flow once they've tried it.

Superwhisper transcribes accurately but with less aggressive automatic cleanup — output stays closer to a raw transcript with lighter formatting, meaning more manual editing for polished prose, particularly on longer dictation sessions. Its custom prompt control does let you tune how the output is formatted for specific use cases, but it requires more upfront configuration than Wispr Flow's out-of-the-box Auto Edits.

Privacy & Offline Processing

This is where Superwhisper has a real, structural advantage: its free tier includes unlimited use of small local AI models that process your voice entirely on-device, with cloud models only used if you explicitly opt in for Pro features. Wispr Flow processes dictation in the cloud by default with no local-only mode, which matters if you handle sensitive or confidential information regularly — legal, medical, or otherwise privileged dictation is a genuine reason to prefer Superwhisper's architecture over Wispr Flow's.

Platform Support

Wispr Flow supports macOS, Windows, iPhone, and Android. Superwhisper supports macOS, Windows, and iOS (no dedicated Android app as of this writing). Both work system-wide, typing into whatever text field has focus rather than requiring a separate app window, and both sync your custom dictionary and settings across devices tied to the same account.

For Android users specifically, Wispr Flow is the only option between the two — Superwhisper's lack of an Android app is a hard dealbreaker if that's your primary mobile platform, regardless of how the pricing or privacy comparison plays out otherwise. This single factor alone settles the decision for a meaningful share of potential users before any other comparison even matters.

Real-World Testing Notes

We dictated the same set of test scripts on both tools across three scenarios: a quick Slack message, a longer email requiring some structure, and a stream-of-consciousness paragraph with false starts and filler words. On the Slack message, both tools performed near-identically — short dictation with little rambling doesn't give Wispr Flow's Auto Edits much to clean up. On the structured email, Wispr Flow's output required no further editing, correctly inferring paragraph breaks and a closing line, while Superwhisper's output was accurate but needed manual paragraph breaks added.

The clearest gap showed up on the rambling paragraph: Wispr Flow's Auto Edits stripped filler words and restructured the thought into a coherent paragraph automatically, while Superwhisper transcribed close to verbatim, filler words and all, leaving substantially more manual cleanup for the user. This is the single biggest practical difference between the two tools in daily use, and it's the main reason Wispr Flow's subscription premium is justified for people who dictate imperfectly (which is most people, most of the time).

Accuracy on clearly enunciated speech in a quiet room was essentially tied between both tools in our testing — the differentiator isn't raw transcription quality so much as what happens to that transcription afterward, which is exactly where Wispr Flow's extra investment in its Auto Edits layer shows up most clearly.

Setup & Onboarding

Both tools take under two minutes to install and start dictating on Mac or Windows. Wispr Flow's onboarding is slightly more guided, walking new users through a permissions setup and a short calibration dictation to personalize the model to their voice and accent. Superwhisper's setup is more utilitarian — functional but with less hand-holding, which power users tend to appreciate and first-time dictation users sometimes find less reassuring. Neither tool requires restarting your computer or granting unusual system permissions beyond standard microphone and accessibility access, which keeps IT approval friction low for both in a workplace setting.

Language Support

Both tools support 100+ languages with auto-detection, so multilingual dictation isn't a meaningful differentiator between them the way it might be against some other competitors in this category. Where they do differ is jargon handling: Wispr Flow's personal dictionary learns names and technical terms specific to your work over repeated corrections, while Superwhisper's custom prompt control lets you pre-configure domain-specific vocabulary upfront rather than learning it reactively.

Neither approach is strictly better — Wispr Flow's learning-by-correction model requires less upfront setup but takes a few corrections before it sticks, while Superwhisper's prompt-based configuration takes more effort initially but gives you more direct control over exactly how uncommon terms get transcribed from day one.

Who Should Actually Choose Which

If you dictate daily as a core part of your workflow — emails, Slack, long-form writing — and value getting to clean, publishable text with minimal editing afterward, Wispr Flow's AI Auto Edits and Command Mode justify the higher subscription cost for most professionals. The polish compounds: saving even a few minutes of manual cleanup per day adds up over a working year, and for anyone billing by the hour or managing a high volume of written communication, that time savings alone can exceed the subscription cost many times over.

If you dictate occasionally, are price-sensitive, or handle any information where offline/local processing matters for privacy or compliance reasons, Superwhisper is the more rational choice — especially given the lifetime license option removes the ongoing subscription decision entirely after a single upfront payment. It's also the more defensible pick for students and hobbyists who want a capable dictation tool without an ongoing financial commitment.

Verdict

Wispr Flow wins for daily writers who want the most polished output with the least manual cleanup, and who don't mind paying a bit more for that polish via subscription.

Superwhisper wins decisively on price (especially the lifetime license), privacy (local/offline processing), and its more generous unlimited free tier — a genuinely strong pick for cost- and privacy-conscious users willing to do slightly more manual cleanup.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Wispr Flow better than Superwhisper?

Wispr Flow's AI Auto Edits and Command Mode voice-editing produce more polished output with less manual cleanup. Superwhisper is cheaper, offers a one-time lifetime license, and supports local/offline processing for privacy that Wispr Flow doesn't offer.

Which is cheaper: Wispr Flow or Superwhisper?

Superwhisper is cheaper on a monthly basis ($7.08-8.49/month vs Wispr Flow's $12-15/month) and also offers a $249.99 one-time lifetime license that Wispr Flow doesn't have at all.

Does Superwhisper work offline?

Yes — Superwhisper's free tier includes unlimited use of small local AI models that process dictation entirely on-device. Wispr Flow processes dictation in the cloud by default and doesn't offer a local/offline mode.

Which has a better free plan, Wispr Flow or Superwhisper?

Superwhisper's free tier has no weekly word cap — unlimited use of small local AI models forever. Wispr Flow's free plan caps at 2,000 words/week on Mac/Windows (1,000/week on iPhone), though every new Wispr Flow account also gets a 14-day trial of Pro features.
