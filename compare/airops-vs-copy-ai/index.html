Bottom Line

AirOps wins for SEO-driven content operations — it chains keyword research, competitor scraping, AI drafting, internal linking, and publishing into one pipeline, and is one of the few tools tracking visibility across ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews, not just Google Search. Copy.ai wins on price and general marketing flexibility — a genuinely unlimited free plan and a broader template library for ads, emails, and social copy that isn't tied to an SEO publishing workflow. The two overlap on the label "workflow automation" but are, in practice, built for genuinely different jobs and different buyers.

What AirOps Does Best

AirOps is built around one core job, and it does that job end to end rather than handling one isolated step: getting a piece of content from keyword idea to published, internally-linked article with as little manual intervention as possible. It chains keyword research, competitor scraping, AI drafting, and internal linking into a single no-code workflow, then publishes directly. Its AI search visibility tracking — monitoring how your content shows up in ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews, not just classic Google rankings — is one of the few tools on the market addressing that specific, increasingly important, gap.

What Copy.ai Does Best

Where AirOps optimizes for a narrow, specialized job done thoroughly, Copy.ai optimizes for breadth and accessibility. Its Workflows feature automates marketing copy production — chaining tasks like research → outline → draft → optimize for ad copy, email sequences, and product descriptions. Its 90+ template library and genuinely unlimited free plan (1 user, no credit card, no expiry) make it far more accessible for solo marketers and small teams who don't need a full SEO publishing pipeline. Brand Voice keeps output consistent across a team without the setup overhead of a dedicated content-ops platform.

The Workflow Stack

These tools solve adjacent but different problems. Teams publishing SEO-driven content at scale — where the bottleneck is keyword research, competitor analysis, and internal linking — get more value from AirOps' end-to-end pipeline. Teams whose bottleneck is repetitive short-form marketing copy (ad variations, email sequences) are better served by Copy.ai's cheaper, more general-purpose Workflows. Some content teams use both: Copy.ai for day-to-day marketing copy, AirOps for the SEO content engine feeding organic traffic.

What Growth-Stage Content Teams Actually Do

Content teams past the early scaling stage tend to run both categories of workflow, but for different jobs. The organic-traffic-driving content — SEO articles, category pages, programmatic landing pages — goes through a dedicated pipeline like AirOps, where competitor research and internal linking are structural requirements, not nice-to-haves, and the $199/month cost is easy to justify against the traffic and revenue that content engine produces over time. Day-to-day marketing copy — a new ad set for a campaign, a product launch email sequence, a batch of social captions — doesn't need that level of research infrastructure, and running it through Copy.ai's cheaper Workflows avoids paying SEO-tool pricing for a job that doesn't require SEO-tool capabilities.

The split matters most as AI answer engines take a growing share of search behavior. Teams relying solely on classic keyword-rank tracking increasingly have a blind spot: content can be ranking well in Google while being invisible or misrepresented in ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Google's AI Overviews. AirOps' visibility tracking across those surfaces is one of the only ways content teams currently get direct signal on that gap — Copy.ai has no equivalent, since it isn't built around search or answer-engine strategy in the first place.

Smaller teams or solo operators without a dedicated SEO function usually don't reach the point where AirOps' specialized pipeline pays for itself, and are better served starting with Copy.ai's free plan and only adding a dedicated SEO content-ops tool once organic content becomes a serious, ongoing growth channel rather than an occasional blog post. A reasonable rule of thumb: if your team is publishing fewer than a handful of SEO-targeted articles per month, the manual research and linking steps AirOps automates aren't yet a real bottleneck worth paying $199/month to remove.

Pricing Deep Dive

AirOps' free plan (1,000 tasks/month) is scoped around workflow steps rather than raw word count, which makes it genuinely hard to compare directly against word-based free plans like Copy.ai's — a single "task" might cover keyword research, a scraped competitor page, or a drafted section, so 1,000 tasks can go quickly for a team running a full keyword-to-published pipeline daily. The $199/month paid tier is priced for teams treating AirOps as their SEO content engine, not as an occasional writing aid, and the cost is usually justified by the hours saved not manually researching keywords, scraping competitors, and manually adding internal links for every article.

Copy.ai's pricing model is far more accessible for individuals: the free plan has no task cap, no word cap, and no expiry — it's genuinely usable indefinitely for a single user. The jump to Starter at $36/month adds 5 seats and more workflow automation headroom, still a fraction of AirOps' $199/month tier. For teams whose content need is mostly marketing copy rather than a full SEO publishing pipeline, Copy.ai is dramatically cheaper for equivalent or greater output volume.

Testing Methodology & Final Verdict

We tested both platforms against a real task: take a target keyword, research the topic, draft a full article, and get it ready to publish with internal links. AirOps handled the entire chain in one configured workflow — scraping the top-ranking competitor pages, drafting a structured outline and full draft, and inserting internal links from our existing site content automatically. Copy.ai's Workflows could replicate the research-to-draft steps with some manual template configuration, but had no equivalent for competitor scraping or automatic internal linking — those steps required manual work outside the tool.

For teams whose growth strategy depends on consistent organic search (and increasingly, AI answer engine) visibility, AirOps' end-to-end automation and cross-platform visibility tracking justify its higher price. For teams whose content need is more general marketing copy — ad variations, email sequences, social captions — Copy.ai's cheaper, more flexible Workflows cover that job well without paying for SEO-specific capabilities that would go unused.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is AirOps better than Copy.ai?

AirOps is better for SEO-focused content operations — it chains keyword research, competitor scraping, drafting, internal linking, and publishing into one automated pipeline, plus tracks visibility in ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews. Copy.ai is better for general marketing copy workflows and has a much more generous free plan.

Which has a better free plan, AirOps or Copy.ai?

Copy.ai's free plan is more generous for individuals — unlimited words for 1 user, free forever. AirOps' free plan is capped at 1,000 tasks per month, which is workflow-based rather than word-based, so the comparison isn't perfectly apples-to-apples.

Can Copy.ai do what AirOps does?

Not fully. Copy.ai's Workflows feature automates marketing copy tasks, but it doesn't have AirOps' SEO-specific pipeline — competitor scraping, keyword-to-published-article automation, and AI search visibility tracking are AirOps-specific capabilities.

How much does AirOps cost compared to Copy.ai?

AirOps costs $0 for 1,000 tasks/month, then $199/month for its main paid tier. Copy.ai costs $0 (unlimited words, 1 user) or $36/month for the Starter plan (5 users). Copy.ai is meaningfully cheaper for small teams; AirOps' pricing reflects its more specialized SEO automation scope.

Does AirOps track visibility in ChatGPT and Perplexity, not just Google?

Yes — this is one of AirOps' distinguishing features. As more search behavior moves to AI answer engines, tracking whether your content is being surfaced (and how it's represented) in ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews is becoming as important as classic keyword rank tracking. Copy.ai has no equivalent feature, since it isn't built around search or content-distribution strategy.
